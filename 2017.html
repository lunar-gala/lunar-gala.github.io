Consumed by our everyday agenda, one can pass by the same places and faces with no concern of the significance of each interaction. Sonder describes the jarring awakening through which we become aware of the parallelism between our own vivid and complex experiences and that of any passerby. No longer droning through life grey and unmoved, every person offers his or her own color, interconnected stories and new realizations. This year's show personifies this realization and aims to lead us through this transitional narrative.

AMBITENDENCY

Jiyoung Ahn, Elaine Choi, Youhyun Jang

Straight and curved, soft and rigid, these incompatible words do coexist around us. Our concept focuses on raveling this boundary between two contrasting elements harmoniously.The line ‘Ambitendency’ is designed to establish different moods of light reflection in different angles. By combining geometrical and organic patterns we will design all the looks with balanced hierarchy.

Assimilate

Ananya Chandra, Ranjini Narayan, Sarika Bajaj

The beauty of being a part of today's world of instant information is that we are exposed to and consumed by a variety of cultures and concepts from all walks of life. Assimilate explores this concept through the lens of our own identities, resulting in an line that is an amalgam of Indian and western concepts as well as engineering and design. Moreover, each outfit revolves around an emotion that represents the conflict between these paradoxical ideas, demonstrating that the differences between these concepts can be viewed as bridges that close their gaps rather than create them.

Blume

Emily Mongilio, Sommer Farber, Mya Snyder

Blume recalls the inherent feminine and romantic themes of lingerie as it brings a new decorative third dimension through the use of silken flowers. The floral theme presents an emphasis on evolutionary cycles, progression, and change. By utilizing sheer materials, we begin to explore the idea of transparency; one cannot truly let another in without being open, vulnerable. Unlike natural flowers, our concept romantically draws upon the idea of everlasting love - flowers that will never die.

de·struo

Brandon Darreff, Zain Islam-Hashmi, Gargi Lagvankar

(v). destroy, demolish, ruin
de + struo (to put together)

Originitating from the Latin word destruam meaning break down, de·struo takes inspiration from the materiality of concrete and uses rockite and plaster to create casted forms that explore the deterioration of gemotry. Exploring a transition from man-made shapes to more natural flows, our line tests the boundaries of casting and blurs the line between construction and fashion. The collection showcases the inevitable transition from geometric and pristine to burned, cracked, and ruined to bring out a new beauty that can be found by putting together the remains of rubble.

états

Joanne Lo, Lynn Kim

"états” plays on the idea of complexity formed by simplicity. Drawing inspiration from the water molecule, we create a line that explores the various states of matter using zip tie modules. These patterns create textures that mimick both the structure and fluidity of water.

Evanesce

Katherine Li, Claire Sun, Ashley Wang

Inspired by Vladimir Nabokov's novel Lolita, "Evanesce" is a wearable art series that portrays a reversed metamorphosis. Each piece in the line represents a role that Lolita experienced along the story: a prey that voluntarily walkes into the trap, a prisoner who eventually flees the captivation, a dead-leaf echo of the nymphet from long ago. With the use of symbols like wire, butterfly, and natural elements, the audience, with the line together, will experience and explore the female role in the current world.

Façade

Katherine Zhao, Samir Gangwani

Façade is an exploration of morphable wearables that resemble the relationships between social identities and self-identifications. By transferring social image of oneself into physical form and replacing it onto human body, we examine the relationships between inner and outer selves. The outer self can be either the social definitions of which we have been carrying on, or we having been pretending to be. The transformation of wearables, triggered by various interactions, serves as gestures to reveal hidden narratives and identities to the audiences.

HUMANESQUE

Emily Hirao, AJ Jeffs, Amy Yang

HUMANESQUE combines circuitry with the human body. We use designs inspired by circuits and use them to create organic shapes resembling different parts of the human body. Some are very easy to distinguish, the heart and lungs, while others are more abstract, like the tendons, muscle. and bones. We use a watercolor technique to add depth and fluidity to the ridged circuit patterns.

LG Dance- Silience

Alexis DeLaRosa, Gilly Johnson

Silience is the kind of unnoticed excellence that carries on around you every day, unremarkably—the hidden talents and slapdash eloquence of fleeting faces. People assume brilliance is a rare and precious quality when it becomes realized, but there still exists the unknown masterpieces and unnoticed jems that may not be flawless but are still somehow perfect, existing all around us.

minuere

Fah Kanjanavanit, Cy Kim

“Minuere” is inspired by the gradual state of change that occurs in various natural phenomena. Rather than portraying a constant concept, our goal is to capture the dynamic and transitional process on the runway. We observed that these gradual state of change occurs in the following three natural phenomena: erosion process than span over millennia, decay of organic matter than span over years, and reproduction in natural systems that span over months. Each piece in our line is inspired by the three different categories.

Smoke

Ruby He, Rachel Muse

Wood represents growth and life. When wood is burned by man, the flames devour its natural beauty and creates dark matter—smoke. The smoke's rapid expansion and consuming nature creates an obscuring condition. Human greed has irreversible consequences, exemplified in the consumption of natural resources. This line questions senseless, destructive human action by contrasting the beauty of wood against the darkness of smoke.

Sonambula

Jonathan D. Ortiz, Taylor Lee Davis

There will come a day when you wake up only to realize that what was once familiar and comfortable to you is now, inexplicably, uncanny. Your environment, your work, everything that used to feel right now instead feels wrong, nebulous, and directionless. Sonambula, Spanish for “sleepwalker,” was born from the state of restless ennui we incurred from the metastasizing divide between the skills we were developing in our classes and our increasingly uncertain visions of the future and our places in it. Sonambula, in turn intends to distort the line between what we view as familiar, comfortable, and real with what we see as exotic, uncanny, and other-- to create outfits that walk the edge of normal and abnormal, shake the habitual, and reject the dissonant stupor that we’ve awoken to.

syn-biosis

Jenny Wong, Christine Zhu

A mutual relationship of dependency, namely symbiosis, occurs between two dissimilar species when one cannot survive without the other. Drawing from this phenomenon, our concept of "syn-biosis" proposes the idea of creating a relationship between the artificial and the biological. The rigid and malleable qualities of our materials allow us to explore varying forms ranging from soft curvatures to styles more edgy and dangerous. This accentuates different external body parts, working with the illusion of translucent tubes feeding into the body in order to convey a visual connection between the synthetic means and the internal bodily mechanisms.

Toska

Lizzy Faeth

Vladimir Nabokov describes toska as both “a sensation of great spiritual anguish, often without any specific cause” and “a longing with nothing to long for.” This line explores modern streetwear in the context of a dystopian universe, reimagining familiar silhouettes to convey the isolation and anguish of societal degradation.

YUCCI

Janice Kim

Y [Shape of the Hanbok]
+ GUCCI [Slang for “Good”]
= YUCCI [“Alluring” in Korean]

The rising popularity of Korean hip hop street fashion borrows heavily from its Western counterpart. The Hanbok, a once ubiquitous piece of traditional Korean clothing, has long since become an anachronism. YUCCI is a fusion of the Hanbok and present-day streetwear, a wearable series that appeals to modern hip hop fashion while also reviving an ever-fleeting Korean tradition.
